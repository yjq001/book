Conversely, the Dow Jones experienced a minor decline (-0.16%), suggesting some profit-taking or sector-specific adjustments amidst overall optimism.

Looking ahead over the next one to two trading days, the market may continue its upward trajectory, buoyed by the tech sector's resilience and ongoing geopolitical optimism. The record closes in major indices suggest strong investor appetite for risk, especially in technology and growth-oriented stocks. However, the slight dip in the Dow highlights underlying caution, possibly related to sector rotation or profit-taking after recent gains.

Key risk factors to monitor include geopolitical developments—particularly the Iran-US negotiations—as any setbacks could introduce volatility. Additionally, US sanctions on Chinese entities involved in Iranian oil trade underscore ongoing geopolitical tensions that could impact energy prices and supply chains. Oil prices have risen sharply for the week, reflecting supply worries, which could influence inflation expectations and market sentiment.

Investors should focus on the evolving geopolitical landscape, sector rotation signals, and macroeconomic indicators such as oil prices and US sanctions developments. Staying alert to potential volatility caused by geopolitical headlines or policy shifts will be crucial. Overall, the market appears to remain in a cautiously bullish phase, but vigilance is advised given the mixed signals and external risk factors.
